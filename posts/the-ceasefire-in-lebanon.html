Israel War Opinion

The ceasefire in Lebanon. Did Iran succeed in tying our hands?

Does Hezbollah even know about the ceasefire?

Ceasefire without a ceasefire.

I responded to that call and agreed to a timeout, or more precisely a temporary ceasefire of 10 days.

This is what Netanyahu said after the ceasefire with Lebanon was signed. There's a catch, though. They signed with Lebanon, not Hezbollah — which continues to strike Israel with its rockets.

Are our hands tied? Well, at least not completely. We continue to eliminate terrorists in southern Lebanon and a bit further, even though we don't bomb Beirut and Dahieh.

Hezbollah is becoming the main threat to Israel now. Ever since it joined the war, Lebanese terrorists haven't left the northern communities alone. They continue striking, again and again and again. It's extremely difficult to live there now. The sirens usually begin to wail only after the first explosions.

I live in Haifa, and it's calm here. I feel ashamed that our people in the North still live in a war. And I can do nothing about it.
