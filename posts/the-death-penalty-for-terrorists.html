The death penalty for terrorists. Justifiable?

Are we closer to Kahanism now?

Execution. Hanging.

The death penalty law for detainees applies to 80% of them. Any person in the West Bank who goes out to kill is mandatorily sentenced to death.

This is what Ben Gvir said. Looks like he won this round.

It's a very complicated issue. I mean, the death penalty in general. Many polls show that the majority of Israelis support it for the terrorists who murdered people. True, I can say that it makes sense, taking into account that many of them get back their freedom as part of deals with terrorist groups.

However, this law in its current state looks like a catastrophe. In general, it can be applied only to Palestinians. No right to appeal. The right to sentence terrorists to life in prison only in exceptional cases.

It's true that the majority of attacks are committed by Palestinians — if we don't consider settlers' violence terrorism. But this law is clearly discriminatory. If we actually apply the death penalty to those who murdered others for political or national reasons, it should be a real trial.

To every terrorist. Every terrorist.

Not because of the international reaction, which will be harsh. But simply because it's our duty to be human beings. To treat others like us.
